My 9-Step UX Design Process

Having a solid UX design process is key to successful product design and development, in addition to fully understanding your user's needs, pain points and goals. The following is my "10 step UX Design Process" that I follow for each UX/UI project I work on.

1. Identify Problem

Identify the problem is the first steps of UX process. I meant it’s very important to have the idea of existing in the market. On the other hand, through research by various new reports and articles to ensure the problem exists. There might have a wide range of similar problem exists.

2. Competitive analysis

A competitive analysis is that thing that addresses us to understand the existing market situation and technology. Conducting a competitive analysis is key to get access to competitors. In addition, it also helps us to find the strength and weakness of competitors.

3. User Interview

Unser interview is kinds of an exam that is taken from user to get the real data and information and overall user experience. On the other hand, to conduct an interview it is better to make few questing regarding the product what will represent the overall idea of the user.

4. User persona

The user persona is done based on the user interview and survey data. To test different app idea, hypotheses it is important to have the user persona. User persona allow as to understand the user pin point, goal and application function.

5. Information Architecture

A good information architecture (IA) helps users to find the information and accomplish the task effectively. Moreover, information architecture is a most important thing of a system that will allow finding the relevant information in certain groups.

6. User Flow

User task flows are important for many perspectives to design a successful user interface. For instance, thinking about the overall app design and specific features before starting the development stage. The user flow allows as to understand the user journey into the application.

7. Sketch/Wireframe

After getting all kinds of data from different sources like survey data, research result, persona pinpoint and so on. It is time to make an initial sketch for the products. The sketch is a quick idea of a product that is done using paper and pencil. Sometimes it is called as low fidelity prototype.

8. Mock-Up

Muck-up present high profile visual prototype design drafts of a product which represent the application of visual structure. Visual elements and functionality in a static way. Visual design is the key point of mock-up which is done based on the wireframing or sketch.

9. Prototype

Making a prototype is always interesting to me because it allows us to see the original feel of a product. In this stage, we can take a quick look at the product including the navigation. Most of the successful product follows to produce a prototype.
